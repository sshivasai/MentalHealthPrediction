About Us

The rise in mental health disorders and the necessity for better medical health treatment have prompted an inquiry into machine learning applications in mental health. A systematic assessment of machine learning approaches to predicting mental health issues is examined in this study. Human mental health is affected by a variety of factors, some of which may include a person's age (depression affects a specific age gap or group), a person's gender (most mental issues affect certain genders more, if not all genders equally), and to some extent, one's family history (depending on one's own upbringing, some people tend to be violent due to childhood trauma). Age, gender, and family history are typically important factors in such diseases. Due to these issues, as well as the subjective nature of age, gender, and family history evaluations, determining whether someone is likely to be mentally sick (having mental issues or problems) or not by simply looking at them becomes extremely difficult, and it is for this reason that certain techniques are used. Various digital methodologies that have been created to demonstrate the possibility of providing precise or goal-oriented evaluations in an efficient manner. In this study, we employ notions involving the employment of a support vector machine (SVM). These are clinical assessment applications that make use of computational learning methods.

The primary goal of SVM is to analyze textual data. It also assesses the algorithm's precision. To support the management of patients with mental problems, the approach for analyzing crucial mental parameters has been well-developed. A classifier has been built to anticipate crucial parameters and make diagnostic decisions by combining the results of age, gender, and family history assessments to provide a final analysis on the mental health issue at hand. Furthermore, we use measures such as accuracy, precision, and recall to analyze the Support vector machine classifier's capacity to predict mental health assessment. As a result, using a mental health predictive model to screen and track mentally ill patients opens up possibilities for evaluation. This contemporary movement aims to improve digital and mobile health technologies.
